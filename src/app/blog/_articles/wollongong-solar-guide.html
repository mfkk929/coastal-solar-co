Why the Illawarra Has Some of the Best Solar Conditions in NSW

Wollongong sits on a narrow coastal shelf between the Illawarra Escarpment and the Tasman Sea. That geography matters more for solar than most people realise. Coastal NSW enjoys strong year-round solar radiation, milder summer heat (panels lose efficiency when they bake past 45°C), and steady cloud movement that rarely parks an overcast layer overhead for days. The net effect is a solar generation profile that slightly beats Sydney and comfortably outperforms inland NSW once you factor in heat derating.

In practical numbers: most Wollongong roofs receive 4.6 to 4.9 peak sun hours per day on average across the year. In mid-summer that climbs to 6+ hours; in the depths of a rainy June week, you might see 2.5. Your annual generation, not any single day, is what pays off your system. For a 6.6kW north-facing array, annual production in Wollongong typically lands between 9,000 kWh and 10,500 kWh — and we've seen above-spec roofs in Kiama, Shellharbour Heights and Gerringong push past 11,000 kWh consistently.

What the Illawarra does not have is the dry, blast-furnace heat of western Sydney or the low winter sun angles of the Southern Highlands. Both of those factors shave output off competing regions. If you're comparing your potential savings to a mate in Penrith or Mittagong, yours will almost certainly be higher per kW installed.

The Escarpment Effect: Which Suburbs Lose Morning Sun

The Illawarra Escarpment is the single biggest local variable. Homes nestled close to the cliffs — particularly in parts of Mount Ousley, Keiraville, Gwynneville, Mount Pleasant, Balgownie, Mount Kembla, Figtree West and some streets in Thirroul and Austinmer — can lose the first 60 to 120 minutes of generation on winter mornings. The cliff line rises sharply to around 500 metres and casts a long eastern shadow from roughly April through August.

This does not kill your return on investment, but it does change the system design. On shaded or partially shaded roofs we design with microinverters (Enphase) or DC optimisers (SolarEdge) so each panel operates independently — rather than dragging the whole string down when one panel is shaded. A string inverter system without optimisation on an escarpment-shaded roof can underperform by 15–25% compared to a well-designed equivalent. This is the number-one reason cheap quotes underperform in the Illawarra.

Suburbs that are generally clear of escarpment shading include most of Wollongong CBD, Fairy Meadow, Coniston, Corrimal, Towradgi, Bulli (east of the Princes Highway), Woonona, Port Kembla, Warrawong, Lake Heights, Kiama, Shellharbour, Oak Flats, and everything south down to Ulladulla. If you're east of the highway, you're usually fine. West of the highway, closer to the mountains — that's where a site-specific shading analysis matters.

Endeavour Energy: Approvals, Export Limits and Delays

Wollongong homes are connected to the Endeavour Energy distribution network, which covers the Illawarra, Shoalhaven and parts of the Southern Highlands. Any grid-connected solar install needs two Endeavour Energy approvals: a pre-approval for the connection application (typically back in 3–10 business days) and a post-install inspection that triggers your formal export permission. Neither is difficult — but both can delay your install if your paperwork is wrong.

Two things worth knowing about Endeavour Energy in 2026. First, export limits. Depending on your street's transformer capacity, Endeavour may cap your solar system's export at 5kW or 10kW — even if your system is larger. This doesn't stop you installing a bigger system; it just means anything above your export limit, that isn't self-consumed, gets clipped. For most Illawarra homes the 10kW export cap is plenty. Some older Port Kembla and Dapto streets are on saturated substations with 5kW caps, which makes battery storage a much stronger play because you can stockpile the excess rather than lose it.

Second, the single-phase vs three-phase distinction. Three-phase homes get higher default export limits, face fewer approval headaches, and can run larger inverters without grid reinforcement. If you're in a three-phase home — common in newer developments in Calderwood, Horsley, Tullimbar, Shell Cove, Kembla Grange and Flinders — you have much more flexibility.

Key fact: On a single-phase 5kW-export-limited street, a 10kW system paired with a 10kWh battery will often outperform a larger 13kW system without storage, because the battery stores what would otherwise be clipped.

System Sizing for Illawarra Homes

A useful starting point for sizing a solar system in Wollongong is your average quarterly electricity bill. As a rough rule, every $500 of quarterly spend translates to roughly 2kW of solar as a minimum useful size. A home paying $800 a quarter benefits from about 6.6kW; a home paying $1,200 a quarter benefits from around 10kW; a home paying $2,000+ a quarter with high daytime use should look at 13kW+ with battery.

The three most common system sizes we install in the Illawarra:

  • 6.6kW system: The entry-level sweet spot. Uses about 18–22 panels, fits on most roofs with room to spare. Generates 9,000–10,500 kWh/year. Installed price $5,500–$6,500 after STC rebate. Typical home: a 3-bedroom Wollongong or Kiama house with 1–2 adults, moderate daytime use.
  • 10kW system: The modern default. 26–32 panels. Generates 13,500–15,000 kWh/year. Installed price $7,800–$9,500 after rebate. Typical home: a 4-bedroom family home with air-con, pool, or an EV charger on the horizon.
  • 10kW + 10kWh battery: The best-economics combo for most homes as of 2026 now that feed-in tariffs have collapsed to 5–8¢/kWh. Generates the same solar as above, but stores evening-use power rather than selling it back for pennies. Installed price $14,000–$17,000 after federal and NSW rebates.

A common mistake Illawarra homeowners make is under-sizing. Panels are cheap; roof space and install labour are the fixed costs. Going from 6.6kW to 10kW typically adds only 30–40% to the price but delivers 50% more energy. If your roof space and your household's daytime consumption support it, the larger system almost always wins on payback.

Suburb-by-Suburb: Quick Local Notes

Here's what we've learned installing across the Illawarra and South Coast:

  • Wollongong CBD / Coniston / Fairy Meadow: Mostly unshaded, single-phase, east-of-highway. Standard installs, fast approvals. 6.6–10kW is the sweet spot.
  • Thirroul / Austinmer / Bulli: Some escarpment shading on western streets. Strong coastal generation. Roofs here often have character tiles — check your installer has tile experience.
  • Keiraville / Mount Keira / Mount Kembla: Escarpment-shaded for 30–90 minutes most mornings. Microinverter or optimiser systems only. Factor 10–15% lower winter generation.
  • Dapto / Lake Illawarra / Albion Park: Some saturated substations with tighter export limits. Three-phase is common in newer estates. Battery makes extra sense where export is limited.
  • Shellharbour / Oak Flats / Shell Cove: Newer housing, often three-phase, excellent solar conditions, strong generation. Usually the smoothest installs.
  • Kiama / Gerringong / Jamberoo: Some of the best unshaded roofs in the state. Our Kiama install average payback is 3.9 years. Sea-air corrosion is a real consideration — specify marine-grade mounting kit.
  • Nowra / Bomaderry / Worrigee: Essential Energy territory from here south. Different approval process, slightly different paperwork timeline. Rural blocks often benefit from larger 10–13kW systems.
  • Berry / Kangaroo Valley / Ulladulla: Longer drives for installers, but roofs are unshaded and performance is excellent. Bushfire considerations in some properties — always confirm BAL ratings with your installer.

Rebates Available to Wollongong Homeowners in 2026

Two main rebate layers apply to residential solar in the Illawarra in 2026:

1. Federal STC rebate. The Small-scale Technology Certificate scheme provides an upfront rebate worth approximately $2,800–$3,400 on a 6.6kW system and $4,200–$5,200 on a 10kW system in Wollongong. This is applied as a point-of-sale discount by your installer — you never see the paperwork. The rebate steps down each year on 1 January and the scheme ends in 2030. A system installed in 2026 receives a higher rebate than the same system installed in 2027. See our complete STC guide for how the certificate count is calculated.

2. NSW Cheaper Home Batteries Program. From mid-2025 the NSW Government has been subsidising residential battery storage for eligible households. Combined with the federal battery rebate introduced in the 2025 federal budget, many Illawarra homes now pay under $6,000 for a 10kWh battery system installed alongside new or existing solar. Eligibility varies by income and property type — we confirm your status at the quoting stage. Read our Cheaper Home Batteries Program explainer for full details.

Some retailers and councils also offer minor additional incentives (interest-free loans, discounted grid connection, etc.), but the two above account for 95% of the rebate value a Wollongong homeowner will receive.

Choosing a Wollongong Solar Installer: What to Look For

We've seen every variety of quote walked into homes from Wollongong up to Ulladulla. The quality differences are genuinely large, and the bad ones are expensive. A good Illawarra solar installer should meet every one of these criteria:

  • CEC-accredited designer on the quote. Not just the installer — the designer, who's the person signing off on your system's engineering. Ask for the accreditation number.
  • A site visit or at minimum a satellite-imagery roof assessment before a firm price is given. Anyone quoting solely from a phone call is guessing.
  • Tier-one panels (Trina, Jinko, LONGi, Q CELLS, REC) — not off-brand panels that disappear after 18 months.
  • Quality inverter (SolarEdge, Enphase, Fronius, Sungrow, GoodWe) with real local support. Be cautious of unknown brands that won't be around for warranty claims.
  • 10-year workmanship warranty, not just the manufacturer's equipment warranty. A 5- or 7-year workmanship warranty is a commercial cost-cutting decision, not a hardware limitation.
  • Published or in-writing pricing. If an installer won't tell you their base price ranges before the in-home sales pitch, that's a red flag.
  • Local physical presence. Installers based outside NSW are common in Wollongong quotes but notoriously hard to chase for post-install issues.
  • Clear explanation of export limits for your specific street. If they say "we don't worry about that" — walk away.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does solar cost in Wollongong in 2026?

A quality 6.6kW system from a CEC-accredited installer in Wollongong costs $5,500–$6,500 after the federal STC rebate is applied. A 10kW system ranges $7,800–$9,500 after rebate. Adding a 10kWh battery takes a 10kW-plus-battery package to $14,000–$17,000 after both federal and NSW rebates. Prices vary with roof type, inverter choice and whether optimisers or microinverters are required for shading.

How long does solar take to pay for itself in Wollongong?

For most Illawarra homes with a good north-facing roof, a 6.6kW or 10kW system pays for itself in 4 to 6 years. High daytime users with air-con, pool pumps or an electric vehicle can see payback under 4 years. Homes with heavy shading or very low daytime consumption may take 6–8 years, which is still well within the 20-plus-year useful life of the panels.

Will my Wollongong roof get enough sun for solar?

The vast majority of Wollongong roofs perform well. North-facing roofs produce the most, but east and west orientations now also work well thanks to improved panel efficiency. Steep-pitched southern roofs are marginal and often better skipped. If your home is close to the escarpment (Keiraville, Mount Keira, parts of Thirroul), morning shading is a real consideration — microinverter or DC-optimiser systems compensate for it effectively.

Do I need Endeavour Energy approval for solar in Wollongong?

Yes. Every grid-connected solar installation in Wollongong requires pre-approval from Endeavour Energy for the grid connection, followed by a post-install inspection. Your installer handles the paperwork — you never interact with Endeavour directly. Pre-approvals typically take 3–10 business days. Some streets have export caps (5kW or 10kW) based on transformer capacity, which your installer should explain during design.

How long does solar installation take in the Illawarra?

The actual rooftop install on a typical 6.6kW or 10kW Wollongong home takes 6–8 hours in one day. The full process from signed contract to switched-on system usually runs 2 to 6 weeks, depending on Endeavour Energy pre-approval, panel and inverter stock, and the meter reconfiguration your retailer performs after install. Should I install battery storage with my Wollongong solar system in 2026?

For most Illawarra homes in 2026, yes — battery has become much more economical now that feed-in tariffs have dropped to 5–8¢/kWh while grid electricity costs 30–34¢/kWh. The NSW Cheaper Home Batteries Program and the federal battery rebate together take $2,000–$4,000 off a 10kWh battery. Can I install solar on a rental property in Wollongong?

Yes, but it requires the property owner's approval and a system designed specifically for the rental scenario. Some landlords in Wollongong are now installing solar to improve rental appeal and charge slightly higher rent. If you're a tenant, you cannot install solar yourself, but you can ask your landlord — more are saying yes as electricity prices keep climbing.
